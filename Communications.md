## 1Ô∏è‚É£ **Plan Communications Management**

### **Goal:**

To create a **Communication Management Plan** that defines *who needs what information, when, how, and by whom it will be delivered.*

---

### **Key Inputs:**

* Project Charter
* Stakeholder Register
* Project Management Plan (esp. Resource & Stakeholder Plans)
* Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEFs)
* Organizational Process Assets (OPAs)

---

### **Tools & Techniques:**

1. **Communication Requirements Analysis**

   * Determines the *information needs* of stakeholders.
   * Helps avoid both *information overload* and *lack of communication*.

   üìò Formula for possible communication channels:
   [
   \text{Number of Channels} = \frac{n(n-1)}{2}
   ]
   Where **n = number of stakeholders**.
   (Example: 5 stakeholders ‚Üí 10 communication channels)

2. **Communication Technology**

   * Medium used: Email, meetings, chat tools, video conferences, reports, dashboards, etc.
   * Selection based on project size, urgency, complexity, and confidentiality.

3. **Communication Models**

   * **Basic Model:** Sender ‚Üí Message ‚Üí Receiver (with feedback loop)
   * **Noise** = Anything that distorts the message (e.g., language barriers, technical issues)

4. **Communication Methods**

   * **Interactive:** Meetings, calls, chats ‚Äî real-time, two-way.
   * **Push:** Sent directly (emails, memos, reports) ‚Äî sender ensures delivery.
   * **Pull:** Stakeholders access when needed (intranet, dashboards).

5. **Meetings & Expert Judgment**

---

### **Outputs:**

* **Communications Management Plan**

  * Includes: stakeholder needs, communication methods, frequency, responsibilities, and escalation process.
* Updates to project documents (stakeholder register, schedule, etc.)

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Manage Communications**

### **Goal:**

Ensure timely collection, creation, distribution, and storage of information.

This is the *execution* part ‚Äî actually **performing communications** based on the plan.

---

### **Key Inputs:**

* Communications Management Plan
* Work Performance Reports
* Enterprise/Organizational factors

---

### **Tools & Techniques:**

* **Communication Technology** (email, dashboards, instant messaging)
* **Project Reporting** (status reports, progress dashboards)
* **Interpersonal and Team Skills**

  * Active listening
  * Conflict resolution
  * Cultural awareness
  * Feedback and persuasion
* **Meetings** (stand-ups, reviews, demos, etc.)

---

### **Outputs:**

* **Project Communications** (the actual deliverables ‚Äî reports, minutes, presentations, etc.)
* Updates to project documents and plans
* **Change requests** (if communication issues affect the project)

---

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ **Monitor Communications**

### **Goal:**

---

### **Key Inputs:**

### **Outputs:**

  * (e.g., Are reports being read? Are issues understood?)
* **Change Requests**

  * (e.g., Adjust frequency or method of communication)
* **Updates to Communication/Stakeholder Management Plans**

## üß† **Best Practices**

* Communicate *early and often*.
* Match communication style to stakeholder preference (visual, verbal, written).
* Use simple, concise language.
* Track communication effectiveness via feedback.
* Escalate issues per the communication plan.
* Keep stakeholders informed ‚Äî surprises create resistance.
