# üß≠ **Project Stakeholder Management**

---

## üéØ **Purpose**

The **Stakeholder Management Knowledge Area** focuses on identifying all people or organizations impacted by the project, understanding their expectations, and developing appropriate strategies to ensure **effective stakeholder engagement** throughout the project lifecycle.

In short:

> ‚ÄúIt‚Äôs about identifying who cares about your project, what they need, and keeping them informed and satisfied.‚Äù

## 1Ô∏è‚É£ **Identify Stakeholders**

### **Goal:**

To identify all people, groups, and organizations affected by the project and analyze their interests, expectations, and potential impact.

---

### **Key Inputs:**

* Project Charter
* Business Documents (Business Case, Benefits Mgmt Plan)
* Agreements, Contracts
* Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEF)
* Organizational Process Assets (OPA)

---

### **Tools & Techniques:**

1. **Stakeholder Analysis** ‚Äì Assess power, influence, and interest.
2. **Data Representation Tools**
   * **Power/Interest Grid**
   * **Influence/Impact Grid**
   * **Salience Model** (Power, Urgency, Legitimacy)
3. **Questionnaires / Surveys / Brainstorming**
4. **Expert Judgment & Meetings**

---

### **Outputs:**

* **Stakeholder Register**
   * The key document listing all stakeholders and details like:
      * Name, role, contact info
      * Level of interest
      * Level of influence
      * Engagement expectations
      * Communication needs

üìò *The Stakeholder Register is the primary output of this process.*

---

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ **Plan Stakeholder Engagement**

### **Goal:**

To develop a **strategy** to engage stakeholders based on their needs, expectations, and potential impact.

---

### **Key Inputs:**

* Project Management Plan
* Stakeholder Register
* EEFs and OPAs

---

### **Tools & Techniques:**

1. **Stakeholder Engagement Assessment Matrix (SEAM)**

   * Compares **current** vs **desired** engagement levels.
   * Engagement levels include:

     * **Unaware** ‚Üí Not aware of project
     * **Resistant** ‚Üí Opposes the project
     * **Neutral** ‚Üí Neither supportive nor resistant
     * **Supportive** ‚Üí In favor of project
     * **Leading** ‚Üí Actively involved or championing

2. **Analytical Techniques**

   * Communication requirement analysis, feedback loops.

3. **Meetings & Expert Judgment**

   * Involve key stakeholders to shape engagement plans.

---

### **Outputs:**

* **Stakeholder Engagement Plan**

  * Defines strategies and actions to promote productive stakeholder involvement.
  * Includes communication methods, frequency, responsible persons, and escalation paths.

---

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ **Manage Stakeholder Engagement**

### **Goal:**

To communicate and collaborate with stakeholders to meet their expectations and resolve issues.

---

### **Key Inputs:**

* Communication Management Plan
* Stakeholder Engagement Plan
* Issue Log
* Change Log

---

### **Tools & Techniques:**

1. **Interpersonal and Team Skills**

   * Active listening
   * Conflict resolution
   * Negotiation
   * Leadership
   * Emotional intelligence

2. **Communication Skills**

   * Tailoring communication to stakeholder preferences.

3. **Ground Rules & Feedback**

   * Setting expectations early and encouraging open dialogue.

4. **Meetings**

   * Regular updates, demos, reviews, etc.

---

### **Outputs:**

* **Change Requests**
  * Due to new requirements or feedback.
* **Issue Log Updates**
* **Stakeholder Engagement Plan Updates**
* **Project Document Updates**

---

## 4Ô∏è‚É£ **Monitor Stakeholder Engagement**

### **Goal:**

To track relationships and ensure stakeholders remain appropriately engaged throughout the project.

---

### **Key Inputs:**

* Issue Log
* Project Communications
* Risk Register
* Work Performance Data

---

### **Tools & Techniques:**

1. **Feedback Collection**

   * Surveys, performance reports, informal discussions.
2. **Observation / Conversation**

   * Informal monitoring of sentiment and engagement.
3. **Stakeholder Analysis Reassessment**

   * Stakeholder power or influence may change during the project.
4. **Meetings / Review Sessions**

---

### **Outputs:**

* **Work Performance Information**

  * Stakeholder satisfaction and engagement trends.
* **Change Requests**

  * If engagement strategies need adjusting.
* **Updates** to Plans and Stakeholder Register.

## üí¨ **Stakeholder Analysis Models**

| Model                   | Description                                         | Use                                                  |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Power/Interest Grid** | Classify stakeholders based on power & interest.    | Helps decide who to manage closely or keep informed. |
| **Salience Model**      | Uses Power, Urgency, Legitimacy.                    | Prioritizes stakeholder attention.                   |
| **Stakeholder Cube**    | Multidimensional model (power, interest, attitude). | Advanced analysis for large projects.                |

## üß† **Tips for Effective Stakeholder Management**

‚úÖ Identify early and update often ‚Äî new stakeholders can appear mid-project.
‚úÖ Build trust through **transparent communication**.
‚úÖ Use **active listening** and **empathy** to understand needs.
‚úÖ Customize communication frequency and method to each stakeholder.
‚úÖ Manage **expectations** before they become issues.
‚úÖ Reassess stakeholder influence regularly (especially in long or complex projects).

## üí° **Example Scenario**

You are managing a city metro construction project:

* Stakeholders include: Government agencies, citizens, suppliers, contractors, and local businesses.
* You identify them using a **power/interest grid**.
* You plan engagement ‚Äî local businesses are **neutral**, but government officials are **leading**.
* You manage them by frequent **status updates** and **community meetings**.
* You monitor feedback and adjust communications if public opinion shifts.
