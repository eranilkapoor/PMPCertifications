# 🧩 **Project Resource Management (PMBOK 6 & 7 Perspective)**

**Purpose:**
To identify, acquire, and manage both *physical* and *human* resources needed to complete the project successfully.

This knowledge area ensures the **right people**, **equipment**, and **materials** are **available at the right time** and **used efficiently**.

---

## 1️⃣ **Plan Resource Management**

**Goal:**
Create a strategy for how to manage project resources (both human & physical).

**Key Inputs:**

* Project Charter
* Project Management Plan (esp. scope & schedule baselines)
* Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEFs)
* Organizational Process Assets (OPAs)

**Tools & Techniques:**

* Expert Judgment
* Data Representation (Responsibility Assignment Matrix – RACI Chart)
* Organizational Theory
* Meetings

**Outputs:**

* **Resource Management Plan**
* **Team Charter**
* Updates to project documents

### 📘 **Key Elements of Resource Management Plan**

* Roles & responsibilities
* Project organization charts
* Staffing management (when & how to acquire/release people)
* Training & recognition plans
* Resource control processes

---

## 2️⃣ **Estimate Activity Resources**

**Goal:**
Estimate *what kind* and *how much* of each resource is needed per activity.

**Inputs:**

* Scope baseline
* Resource calendars
* Risk register
* Cost estimates

**Tools & Techniques:**

* Bottom-up estimating
* Analogous & Parametric estimating
* Alternatives analysis
* Published estimating data
* Meetings

**Outputs:**

* Resource requirements (type, quantity, skill level)
* Resource breakdown structure (RBS)
* Basis of estimates
* Updates to documents

---

## 3️⃣ **Acquire Resources**

**Goal:**
Obtain necessary team members, equipment, materials, and facilities.

**Inputs:**

* Resource management plan
* Procurement management plan
* Resource calendars

**Tools & Techniques:**

* Negotiation (with functional managers or vendors)
* Pre-assignment (if known in advance)
* Acquisition from internal/external sources
* Virtual teams (if distributed)

**Outputs:**

* Physical resource assignments
* Project team assignments
* Resource calendars
* Change requests

💡 *Tip:* The PM ensures **resources are committed** when needed—not just requested.

---

## 4️⃣ **Develop Team**

**Goal:**
Improve team performance, competencies, and motivation.

**Focus:**
*Team building, training, and engagement.*

**Tools & Techniques:**

* Colocation (bring people together physically/virtually)
* Team-building activities (Tuckman’s stages: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning)
* Recognition and rewards
* Training & coaching
* Individual and team assessments

**Outputs:**

* Team performance assessments
* Change requests
* Updates to enterprise and project documents

---

## 5️⃣ **Manage Team**

**Goal:**
Monitor team performance, resolve issues, and enhance communication.

**Inputs:**
* Issue Log
* Project Team Assignments 
* RACI Chart
* Team Charter

**Tools & Techniques:**

* Observation and conversation
* Conflict management (problem-solving, collaborating, compromising, avoiding, accommodating, forcing)
* Project performance appraisals
* Emotional intelligence

**Outputs:**

* Change requests
* Updates to issue log & lessons learned
* Updates to team assignments

**Team Progress**

**Burndown Chart**

**Burnup Chart**

🧠 *Key Skill:* The PM should demonstrate **emotional intelligence (EI)** and **situational leadership** — adapting management style to the team’s maturity level.

---

## 6️⃣ **Control Resources**

**Goal:**
Ensure physical resources are available and used as planned, monitor for over/underutilization.

**Inputs:**

* Agreements / Contracts
* Work Performance Data 
* Project Management Plan

**Tools & Techniques:**

* Performance Reviews
* Resource optimization (leveling & smoothing)
* Trend analysis (check resource usage over time)
* Alternative Analysis
* Project Management Infromation System (PMIS)
* Problem solving

**Outputs:**

* Work performance information
* Change requests
* Updates to resource and project documents
* Project Management Plan

**Project Documents:**
* Impediment Board
* Issue Log
* Lessons Learned Register
* Resource Assignments
* Project Schedule
* Resource Breakdown Structure
* Resource Requirements
* Risk Register

## 📊 **Important Tools & Diagrams**

| Tool                                   | Description                                                                                    |
| -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **RACI Matrix**                        | Responsibility Assignment Matrix showing who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed. |
| **Resource Histogram**                 | Bar chart showing resource usage per time period.                                              |
| **Resource Breakdown Structure (RBS)** | Hierarchical chart grouping resources by type/category.                                        |
| **Tuckman’s Model**                    | Team development stages: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning.               |
| **Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs**        | Motivation theory – physiological → safety → belonging → esteem → self-actualization.          |
| **Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory**       | Motivators (achievement) vs. Hygiene factors (salary, policies).                               |

---

## ⚙️ **Resource Optimization Techniques**

| Technique              | Use                                                                         |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Resource Leveling**  | Adjust schedule to resolve resource conflicts (may change critical path).   |
| **Resource Smoothing** | Adjust activities to meet resource limits *without* changing critical path. |

## 💡 **Key Exam Tip:**

* **Human resources** → Focus on people, leadership, motivation.
* **Physical resources** → Focus on materials, equipment, facilities.
* Always consider **organizational structure** (functional, matrix, projectized) — it affects how much authority the PM has over resources.
